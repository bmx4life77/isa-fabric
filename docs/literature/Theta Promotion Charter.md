# Θ (Theta) Promotion Charter

### *Canonical, Normative, and Binding Across ISA Fabric & ISA Metrics GCS*

---

## Preamble

Θ (Theta) was introduced into the ISA universe to give **identity** to temporal, oscillatory, and spectral semantics that already existed implicitly within system behavior.

Θ was not created to command, optimize, or enforce.

Θ was created to **reveal**.

This Charter exists to ensure that Θ may *never* exceed that purpose, even as the system evolves.

---

## Article I — Ontological Status

1. Θ is defined as a **Lens-Class Operator**.
2. Θ does not represent system state.
3. Θ does not constitute governance authority.
4. Θ does not prescribe actions, thresholds, or outcomes.

These properties are **invariant** across all versions.

---

## Article II — Promotion Philosophy

1. Promotion of Θ is **optional**, not mandatory.
2. Non-promotion of Θ is a **complete and valid outcome**.
3. Promotion must **never** collapse the separations of:

   * State
   * Dynamics
   * Interpretation
4. Promotion must **never** grant Θ direct or indirect authority.

Promotion exists to clarify interpretation — not to create power.

---

## Article III — Promotion Levels (Exhaustive)

Θ may exist only in the following recognized levels:

### **L0 — Lens-Only (Default)**

* Θ is advisory
* Θ is observational
* Θ is read-only
* Θ informs interpretation only

### **L1 — Confidence Modulator**

* Θ may modulate uncertainty or confidence weighting
* Θ may influence how strongly interpretations are held
* Θ may not gate, block, or trigger actions

### **L2 — Governance-Referenced (Narrative Only)**

* Θ-regime may be referenced in governance narratives
* Θ may support explanation and contextualization
* Θ may not determine outcomes

### **L3 — Prohibited**

* Θ as a gate, threshold, target, or authority
* Θ as a pass/fail condition
* Θ as an optimization objective

**L3 does not exist and shall never exist.**

Any proposal implying L3 is constitutionally invalid.

---

## Article IV — Promotion Preconditions

Θ may only be considered for promotion if **all** of the following are satisfied:

1. Demonstrated epistemic maturity across multiple regimes
2. Proven resistance to optimization or gaming
3. Stability under noise and adversarial conditions
4. Clear improvement in governance interpretation
5. No dependency of governance or FRE on Θ’s presence

Failure of **any** condition blocks promotion without penalty.

---

## Article V — Anti-Optimization Clause

1. Θ shall never be an optimization target.
2. Any proposal claiming to:

   * maximize Θ
   * minimize Θ
   * directly improve Θ
     is semantically invalid.
3. Θ exists to be *observed*, not *pursued*.

This clause is permanent.

---

## Article VI — Sibling Symmetry Requirement

1. Any Θ promotion must be mirrored identically in:

   * ISA Fabric
   * ISA Metrics GCS
2. No asymmetric authority may arise between siblings.
3. Θ may never be promoted in one sibling without the other.

Fabric and GCS remain:

* independent
* aligned
* non-collusive

---

## Article VII — Reversibility

1. Any promotion of Θ must be reversible.
2. Reversal of promotion must not destabilize:

   * governance
   * FRE
   * system interpretation
3. If reversal is unsafe, promotion is invalid.

Θ must never become indispensable.

---

## Article VIII — The Non-Promotion Clause

> **Θ remaining permanently at L0 is a valid, complete, and successful endpoint.**

There is:

* no decay
* no pressure
* no expectation of escalation

The system is considered whole even if Θ is never promoted.

---

## Article IX — Constitutional Closure

This Charter binds Θ permanently to its role as a **lens**, not a lever.

Θ may grow richer internally.
Θ may grow wiser over time.
Θ may never grow authoritarian.

This Charter supersedes all informal interpretations.
