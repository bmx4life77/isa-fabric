## Hyperbolic cosine and hyperbolic secant as dual curvature profiles

The two functions:

\[
\cosh(x) = \frac{e^x + e^{-x}}{2}
\]

\[
\text{sech}(x) = \frac{1}{\cosh(x)}
\]

are not just reciprocals — they are **geometric duals**.

### How they behave:

- **cosh(x)** grows outward symmetrically from a minimum at \(x = 0\).  
  It is a *curvature‑expanding* function.

- **sech(x)** shrinks inward symmetrically from a maximum at \(x = 0\).  
  It is a *curvature‑contracting* function.

They are mirror images in the sense of:

- one is convex upward  
- the other is convex downward  
- one expands curvature  
- the other compresses curvature  
- one is maximal at the edges  
- the other is maximal at the center  

This is exactly the kind of **dual behavior** that ISA Fabric uses when measuring:
