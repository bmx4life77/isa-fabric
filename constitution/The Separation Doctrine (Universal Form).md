## **ARTICLE XVII.0 — THE SEPARATION PRINCIPLE**

### **XVII.0.1 — Universal Prohibition**

> **No interpretive signal may influence the enforcement mechanism that validates interpretations.**

This principle applies to:
- Θ-family operators (temporal/spectral interpretation)
- β (systemic risk interpretation)
- ι (momentum interpretation)
- φ (efficiency interpretation)
- Dashboards (visual interpretation)
- Narratives (social interpretation)
- Expert opinions (human interpretation)
- Any signal that **describes** rather than **prescribes**

### **XVII.0.2 — The Circular Collapse Theorem**

If an interpretive signal `I` influences an enforcement mechanism `E` that validates `I`:

```
I → E → validates(I) → I' → E' → validates(I') → ...
```

This creates:
1. **Runaway feedback** (interpretation strengthens enforcement strengthens interpretation)
2. **False convergence** (system appears stable while drifting)
3. **Adversarial exploitation** (attackers manipulate I to control E)
4. **Phase space collapse** (system loses ability to distinguish states)
5. **Constitutional capture** (enforcement becomes interpretation-dependent)

**Constitutional Consequence:**  
Any governance action dependent on such a loop is **void ab initio** (void from the beginning).

### **XVII.0.3 — The Epistemic Boundary**

Governance systems must maintain strict separation between:

| Domain | Role | Examples | Authority Level |
|--------|------|----------|-----------------|
| **Descriptive** | Observation, interpretation, diagnosis | Θ, β, ι, φ, dashboards, narratives | **None** (advisory only) |
| **Normative** | Enforcement, gating, authority | ψ₅, SE, constitutional articles | **Absolute** (binding) |

**Crossing this boundary constitutes a constitutional violation.**

## **ARTICLE XVII.1 — INTERPRETIVE SIGNAL CLASSIFICATION**

### **XVII.1.1 — Signal Classes**

All governance signals must be classified into exactly one category:

#### **Class A: Normative Signals (Authoritative)**
- **Definition**: Signals that may directly influence execution state
- **Requirements**:
  - Constitutional authorization (Article 0)
  - Metric Authority Doctrine compliance
  - BIV verification
  - Non-regression guarantees
- **Current members**: ψ₅, SE
- **Expansion**: Prohibited without BEP + constitutional amendment

#### **Class B: Interpretive Signals (Advisory)**
- **Definition**: Signals that describe system state but cannot influence execution
- **Characteristics**:
  - Observational only
  - May inform human understanding
  - May support narrative/explanation
  - **Cannot gate, trigger, or block actions**
- **Current members**: Θ, β, ι, φ, VU, RSI, drift metrics, divergence metrics

#### **Class C: Prohibited Signals (Invalid)**
- **Definition**: Signals that attempt to blur A/B distinction
- **Examples**:
  - "Advisory gates" (Class B pretending to be Class A)
  - "Context-dependent enforcement" (Class A modulated by Class B)
  - "Soft thresholds" (Class B used as tie-breakers)
- **Status**: Constitutionally invalid; proposals referencing them auto-fail

### **XVII.1.2 — Classification Immutability**

Once a signal is classified:
- **Class A** signals cannot be demoted without constitutional crisis
- **Class B** signals cannot be promoted without Article XVII.2 process
- **Class C** signals cannot be legitimized under any circumstances
