# **1. Constitutional Summary**

This BEP establishes the **IMSI Attribution & Observability Layer** as a constitutional subsystem built on top of:

- Π_society  
- Σ_society(t)  
- IMSI(t)  
- Article XXX (Societal Telemetry Doctrine)  

It introduces:

- **Attribution Vector A(t)**  
- **Delta Attribution ΔIMSI(t)**  
- **Causal Attribution Chain**  
- **Societal Observability Requirements**  

This BEP defines how IMSI is decomposed, attributed, and exposed to operators and governance systems in a deterministic, replay‑verifiable, and envelope‑aware manner.

---

# **2. Motivation**

IMSI (BEP‑v6‑003) provides a **single societal index**, but governance requires:

- decomposition  
- attribution  
- sensitivity analysis  
- delta tracking  
- observability  
- interpretive foresight  

Without attribution, IMSI would be a black box.  
Without observability, governance would lack transparency.  
Without delta attribution, operators could not diagnose societal shifts.

This BEP provides the **constitutional machinery** for societal diagnostics.

---

# **3. Specification (Constitutional Layer)**

## **3.1 Attribution Vector A(t)**

\[
A(t) = [a_1, ..., a_6], \quad a_i = w_i \cdot x_i(t)
\]

Where:

- \(x_i(t)\) are normalized societal components extracted via Π_society  
- \(w_i\) are versioned constitutional weights  
- \(a_i\) are attributed contributions  

A(t) MUST be:

- deterministic  
- normalized  
- replay‑verifiable  
- versioned  

---

## **3.2 IMSI Decomposition**

\[
IMSI(t) = 100 \cdot \sum a_i
\]

This decomposition MUST:

- preserve monotonicity  
- preserve normalization  
- preserve lineage  
- remain drift‑resistant  

---

## **3.3 Delta Attribution**

\[
\Delta IMSI(t) = \sum w_i \cdot (x_i(t) - x_i(t-1))
\]

ΔIMSI(t) MUST:

- be deterministic  
- be lineage‑linked  
- be replay‑verifiable  
- be envelope‑aware  

ΔIMSI SHALL be used for:

- societal volatility detection  
- governance alerts  
- envelope S‑axis adjustments  

---

## **3.4 Causal Attribution Chain**

```
Event → Σ(t) → Σ_society(t) → IMSI(t) → Attribution
```

This chain MUST be:

- deterministic  
- causal  
- replay‑verifiable  
- lineage‑preserving  

No layer may bypass the previous one.

---

## **3.5 Observability Requirements**

The system SHALL expose:

- **contributions** (a_i)  
- **delta contributions** (x_i(t) − x_i(t−1))  
- **sensitivity** (∂IMSI/∂x_i)  
- **confidence** (versioned, deterministic)  

Observability MUST NOT:

- reveal private data  
- expose raw Σ(t) components  
- bypass Π_society  

---

## **3.6 Integration with Article XXX**

This BEP extends Article XXX by defining:

- attribution as a constitutional requirement  
- societal observability as a governance‑facing subsystem  
- deterministic decomposition rules  
- replay‑verifiable societal diagnostics  

---

## **3.7 Quantum Alignment Constraints**

Quantum interpretive layers MAY annotate attribution but SHALL NOT:

- mutate A(t)  
- mutate ΔIMSI(t)  
- override IMSI  
- bypass Π_society  

If κ_align < κ_min:

- attribution SHALL be marked “low confidence”  
- societal volatility SHALL be treated conservatively  
- envelope S‑axis MAY be downgraded

# **4. Technical Appendix**

## **4.1 Attribution Computation Pipeline**

1. Π_society extracts societal signals  
2. Signals normalized → x_i(t)  
3. Weighted → a_i  
4. Summed → IMSI(t)  
5. Differenced → ΔIMSI(t)  
6. Exposed → Observability Layer  

---

## **4.2 Sensitivity Analysis**

\[
\frac{\partial IMSI}{\partial x_i} = 100 \cdot w_i
\]

Sensitivity MUST be:

- deterministic  
- versioned  
- replay‑verifiable

## **4.3 Envelope Integration**

IMSI attribution influences:

- **S‑axis** (security) when societal volatility is high  
- **E‑axis** (environmental/societal) directly  
- **P‑axis** indirectly via stability constraints  

Forbidden combinations apply.
