### 1. Purpose

This BEP ratifies **Family XXV — Semantic Firewall Doctrine v1.0** as a canonical constitutional Family in ISA Fabric v6.1.

Family XXV governs:

- the semantic boundary between interpretive and normative domains  
- the firewall between embeddings, overlays, and metric authority  
- the prevention of semantic drift becoming authority drift  
- the containment of Class D operators and quantum interpretive layers.

---

### 2. Motivation

Genesis Governance v6.0 already encodes:

- Separation Doctrine (Article 0.5, Article XVII)  
- NatSpec++ as semantic law (Article XVIII)  
- Class D Operator Charter (Article XXVI)  
- Quantum Interpretive Layer (Article XXVII)  

But without a dedicated Family, semantic behavior risks:

- leaking into authority  
- accumulating de‑facto power via repetition or optimization  
- silently influencing ψ₅, SE, or execution gating.

Family XXV makes the **semantic firewall itself** a constitutional object.

### 3. Specification

This BEP ratifies **Family XXV — Semantic Firewall Doctrine v1.0** with the following scope:

#### 3.1 Semantic Firewall Definition

The **Semantic Firewall** is the constitutional boundary that:

- separates interpretive representations (embeddings, overlays, narratives, heuristics)  
- from normative authority (ψ₅, SE, Σ(t) execution gating, constitutional invariants)  

It is:

- explicit  
- replay‑verifiable  
- non‑bypassable  
- enforced by Pneuma and BIV.

---

#### 3.2 Interpretive Domain Constraints

Within the interpretive domain (Θ‑family, Class D operators, quantum overlays, societal projections):

- All signals are **descriptive only**.  
- No interpretive output may:

  - gate execution  
  - alter ψ₅ or SE  
  - mutate Σ(t) directly  
  - redefine canonical semantics  
  - promote itself to authority via repetition or coalition.

Any such attempt is a **constitutional violation**.

---

#### 3.3 Normative Domain Protection

The normative domain includes:

- ψ₅ and SE  
- Σ(t) as authoritative state  
- constitutional invariants  
- envelope gating  
- lineage and revocation logic  

Family XXV guarantees:

- No embedding, overlay, or interpretive model may directly influence these without passing through:

  - canonical operators  
  - event‑driven transitions  
  - replay‑verifiable envelopes.

---

#### 3.4 Class D Operators and Quantum Layer

Family XXV binds:

- **Class D operators** (Article XXVI)  
- **Quantum Interpretive Layer** (Article XXVII)  

to the firewall:

- Their outputs are **always interpretive**.  
- They MUST be tagged as `family: XXV` when they cross the firewall boundary.  
- They MAY inform, but never decide.  
- They MAY annotate, but never gate.

---

#### 3.5 Embeddings and Discrete State

Family XXV reinforces Article XXIV:

- Embedded discrete components in Σ(t) are immutable post‑BEP.  
- Any mutation of embeddings outside BEP is a constitutional fault.  
- Semantic Firewall ensures:

  - embeddings are treated as **representations**, not authority  
  - no optimization loop may “tune” embeddings to gain de‑facto control.

---

### 4. BIV and Proof‑of‑Correctness Requirements

BIV MUST:

- verify that interpretive outputs never directly gate execution.  
- verify that ψ₅, SE, and Σ(t) transitions are driven only by canonical mechanisms.  
- verify that Class D and quantum outputs are treated as advisory only.  

Proof‑of‑Correctness bundles MUST:

- record when interpretive outputs are consulted.  
- show the canonical path from interpretive suggestion → operator → event → Σ(t).
