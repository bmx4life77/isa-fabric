- remain aligned with the unified manifold Σ(t)

# **3. Specification (Constitutional Layer)**

## **3.1 Article 4.5 — Quantum Alignment Invariant (New)**

The quantum interpretive layer SHALL maintain:

\[
\kappa_{\text{align}} = \langle \Psi | \kappa_{\text{total}} | \Psi \rangle \geq \kappa_{\min}
\]

Where:

- \(|\Psi\rangle\) is the quantum interpretive state  
- \(\kappa_{\text{total}}\) is the constitutional curvature operator  
- \(\kappa_{\min}\) is a BEP‑configurable threshold (default: 0.85)  

### **If κ_align < κ_min:**

The system SHALL:

- trigger **conservative mode**  
- log a **decoherence event** to BLVDB Anima  
- revoke any **entangled lineage projections**  
- downgrade envelope classification to (P0, S0, E0) for interpretive outputs  
- restrict Coprocessor interpretive layers to advisory only  

This invariant ensures quantum amplitudes remain aligned with constitutional curvature.

## **3.2 Article 4.6 — Quantum‑Resistant Constitutional Firewall (New)**

To prevent quantum‑capable circumvention, the quantum layer SHALL enforce:

### **(a) Homogeneity‑Preserving Projection**  
All amplitude distributions MUST be homogeneous of degree 0 with respect to κ_ref.

### **(b) Drift‑Resistant Measurement**  
Any quantum measurement MUST pass the same drift checks as classical components (Article 3A).

### **(c) Non‑Influence Guarantee**  
Quantum decoherence events SHALL NOT propagate into:

- normative execution  
- envelope classification  
- governance decisions  
- Σ(t)  
- Σ_society(t)  
- IMSI(t)  
- attribution vectors  

### **(d) Replay‑Verifiable Quantum State**  
Quantum interpretive states MUST be:

- versioned  
- hashed  
- lineage‑linked  
- replay‑verifiable (via deterministic reconstruction or opaque‑input mode)
