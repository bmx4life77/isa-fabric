### 1. Purpose

This BEP ratifies **Family XXIV — Psi‑Propagation Doctrine v1.0** as a canonical constitutional Family in ISA Fabric v6.1.

Family XXIV governs:

- how ψ‑class signals (especially ψ₅) propagate through Σ(t)  
- how influence, pressure, and risk signals travel across components  
- how propagation is bounded, observable, and replay‑verifiable  
- how ψ‑flows avoid becoming hidden authority channels.

---

### 2. Motivation

ψ₅ and related ψ‑signals are central to:

- metric authority  
- safety envelope behavior  
- emergency triggering  
- resilience assessment  

Without a dedicated Family, ψ‑flows risk:

- becoming implicit  
- leaking across boundaries  
- accumulating de‑facto authority  
- escaping replay visibility.

Family XXIV makes ψ‑propagation **explicit, bounded, and constitutional**.

### 3. Specification

This BEP ratifies **Family XXIV — Psi‑Propagation Doctrine v1.0** with the following scope:

#### 3.1 ψ as Constitutional Field

- ψ‑signals (including ψ₅) are treated as **fields over Σ(t)**, not ad‑hoc variables.  
- All ψ‑related state MUST be:

  - represented in Σ(t)  
  - derivable from event lineage  
  - replay‑verifiable.

No ψ‑state may exist off‑ledger.

---

#### 3.2 Propagation Invariants

Family XXIV establishes:

- **Bounded Propagation:** ψ‑signals MAY propagate only along explicitly defined constitutional channels (e.g., operator outputs, envelope transitions, mitosis boundaries).  
- **No Silent Amplification:** ψ‑magnitude MAY NOT increase without a recorded, constitutional cause (e.g., event, operator, or envelope transition).  
- **No Hidden Sinks:** ψ‑signals MAY NOT be absorbed or nullified without explicit, replay‑verifiable events.

---

#### 3.3 ψ and Safety Envelopes

- ψ₅ remains a **metric authority signal** (per Article 0 and Article II).  
- ψ‑propagation MUST respect:

  - Safety Envelope (SE) geometry  
  - hysteretic thresholds  
  - temporal windows (Family XXIII)  

- Any ψ‑driven transition that bypasses envelope checks constitutes a **constitutional fault**.

---

#### 3.4 Cross‑Axis and Cross‑Family Constraints

- ψ‑signals MAY influence:

  - risk assessment  
  - envelope tightening  
  - conservative mode activation  

but MAY NOT:

- override metric truth  
- introduce new authority sources  
- bypass Separation Doctrine (Article 0.5).

Cross‑Family ψ‑flows MUST:

- be explicitly defined  
- be tagged with Family XXIV semantics  
- be visible in replay and BIV inspection.

---

#### 3.5 Integration with Σ(t) and Event Stratification

- ψ‑state is part of Σ(t) and MUST be reconstructible via:

  \[
  \Sigma(t) = \text{Reduce}(\text{Events}_{0 \to t})
  \]

- ψ‑related transitions MUST appear in:

  - Execution Layer (operator_execution)  
  - Attributed Transition Layer (hysteron_transition / embedded_transition)  
  - Primitive Transition Layer (raw_transition)  

No ψ‑change may occur outside this stratification.
