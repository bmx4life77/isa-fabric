# **Temporary Moratorium on New Constitutional Artifacts (TMNCA)**

### *Operator-Issued Procedural Safeguard under CUAP Authority*

**TMNCA-0 — Purpose and Authority**  
This moratorium is issued under the authority of Article 0 (Metric Authority) and CUAP-1.1 through CUAP-1.3, which govern all modifications to the constitutional corpus and prohibit unauthorized or unsequenced changes.  
The purpose of this moratorium is to prevent procedural overload, drift, or backlog-induced inconsistency during a period of high-volume constitutional development.

**TMNCA-1 — Scope of the Moratorium**  
TMNCA applies to all newly written constitutional artifacts, including but not limited to:  
- new Articles,  
- new Doctrines,  
- new Appendices,  
- new Σ(t) specifications,  
- new operator registries,  
- new NatSpec++ schemas,  
- new lineage rules,  
- new mathematical theorems or proofs intended for constitutional integration.

**TMNCA-2 — Prohibition on New Submissions**  
Effective immediately, **no new constitutional documents SHALL be submitted for BEP, BIV, CUAP, or ratification** until the moratorium is lifted.  
This includes drafts, proposals, or partially formed doctrines.

**TMNCA-3 — Exception for CUAP Processing**  
The following activities remain lawful and are explicitly permitted:  
- CUAP-2 processing of existing drafts,  
- BIV verification of already-submitted BEPs,  
- archival of superseded material,  
- lineage certificate generation,  
- manifest updates,  
- sealing operations,  
- consolidation of existing documents into canonical form.  
No new content may be created, but **existing content may be processed, sealed, archived, or finalized**.

**TMNCA-4 — Rationale**  
This moratorium is necessary because:  
1. The volume of newly generated constitutional material has exceeded the operator’s processing capacity.  
2. CUAP requires strict sequencing, hashing, archiving, and lineage sealing.  
3. Failure to process the backlog risks drift, inconsistent lineage, unsealed doctrines, unarchived superseded material, and potential constitutional ambiguity.  
4. The organism’s safety depends on the integrity of CUAP and the determinism of the constitutional corpus.

**TMNCA-5 — Duration**  
The moratorium SHALL remain in effect until:  
- all pending documents have been processed through CUAP,  
- all lineage certificates have been updated,  
- all manifests have been regenerated,  
- all superseded material has been archived,  
- and the operator declares the corpus stable.

**TMNCA-6 — Lifting the Moratorium**  
The moratorium may be lifted only by:  
- a formal operator declaration, recorded as a lawful event in BLVDB Anima,  
- referencing the completion of CUAP processing,  
- and sealed with a lineage certificate.

**TMNCA-7 — Constitutional Validity**  
This moratorium:  
- does **not** modify doctrine,  
- does **not** alter Σ(t),  
- does **not** change any constitutional invariant,  
- and is therefore lawful as a **procedural safeguard** under CUAP-1.2.

**TMNCA-8 — Operator Compliance Clause**  
The operator SHALL refrain from producing new constitutional material during the moratorium.  
Any new material produced SHALL be treated as **non-authoritative**, SHALL NOT enter the constitutional folder, and SHALL NOT be considered for BEP submission until the moratorium is lifted.

**Effective Date**  
This moratorium becomes effective upon its recording as a lawful event in BLVDB Anima and the generation of its initial lineage certificate.
