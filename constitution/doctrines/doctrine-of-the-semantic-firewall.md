# ⭐ **2. Doctrine of the Semantic Firewall**  
*(Constitutional Doctrine — Interpretive/Narrative Containment)*

**Definition:**  
This Doctrine establishes the constitutional firewall that prevents semantic signals (activation, release, lineage facts, interpretive instruments) from acquiring normative authority or influencing Class A decisions.

**Principles:**  
1. **Interpretive Containment:** Semantic signals are Class B and may never cross into Class A.  
2. **Semantic Immunity:** The system must detect and neutralize semantic overreach.  
3. **Non‑Normativity:** Semantic facts may describe behavior but may not justify governance actions.
