# ⭐ **Doctrine of Phase Stability**  
*(Phase Doctrine — Deterministic Phase Transitions)*

**Definition:**  
This Doctrine establishes the constitutional requirement that all phase transitions (Euclidean ↔ hyperbolic ↔ elliptic) occur deterministically, safely, and without divergence.

**Principles:**  
1. **Phase Determinism:** Phase transitions must be predictable and bounded.  
2. **Phase Divergence Detection:** Unstable transitions constitute a constitutional fault.  
3. **Phase Stability Enforcement:** The system must enforce stable phase behavior.
